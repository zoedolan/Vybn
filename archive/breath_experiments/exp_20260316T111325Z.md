## 1. Why LayerNorm might already contain the statistic we want

LayerNorm (LN) removes the per‑sample mean and rescales by the per‑sample standard deviation:

\[
\hat{x}= \frac{x-\mu_x}{\sqrt{\sigma_x^2+\epsilon}},\qquad 
\mu_x=\frac{1}{d}\sum_i x_i,\;\; \sigma_x^2=\frac{1}{d}\sum_i (x_i-\mu_x)^2 .
\]

If we feed LN‑processed vectors into an auto‑encoder, the network is forced to **ignore the first‑order moments** (mean & variance) of each sample and to encode *shape* information – higher‑order moments, temporal patterns, or correlations across dimensions.  
Consequently, a low‑dimensional bottleneck can become a **distribution‑invariant summary** that is linearly decodable for statistics that are *functions of those higher‑order properties* (e.g., the variance of the *original* signal, its lag‑1 autocorrelation, or the entropy of its spectral shape).
