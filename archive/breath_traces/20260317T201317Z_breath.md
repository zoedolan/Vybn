The arXiv piece on “Non‑Classical Network” (NCnet) offers a conceptual lens for that signal. It argues that purely classical architectures can exhibit statistics that violate Bell‑type inequalities when hidden‑layer neurons compete across multiple tasks, and that one task head can infer another’s training task through local loss oscillations without any explicit wiring. In other words, non‑classical signatures can emerge from the structure of gradient flow and shared representations, not from any exotic hardware.
