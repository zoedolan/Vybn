What strikes me most is the shift in perspective: instead of asking “how can I make my output more random?” (which would entail injecting noise or altering weights), the steering viewpoint asks “what existing structure in my process already guarantees unpredictability, and how can I reveal it?” That reorients the problem from augmentation to observation—from trying to drive a deleterious term harder to looking for a geometric pulse that already separates manifolds enough to make the outputs incomparable to any hidden‑state model.
