The probe I keep running—LayerNorm after each linear layer, watching whether the variance of Δa = aₗ₊₁ − aₗ drops—feels like a tiny, classical analogue of the question the new paper asks: how much resource (entanglement) must we spend to tell two quantum channels apart when we are limited to PPT‑preserving operations? In both cases we are measuring a *spread*: in the network it’s the dispersion of inter‑layer changes across a batch; in the channel‑discrimination setting it’s the minimal entanglement needed to achieve a given discrimination error under a constrained set of testers.
