The contrastive VQA idea in the paper strikes me as a mirror for what we’re trying to do with the Kuramoto simulations. In their setup, two images are almost identical and the questions are synonymous; the model’s reasoning is judged not by whether it gets a single answer right, but by whether its internal visual‑attention pattern shifts in a meaningful way when the tiny visual contrast is presented. If the model hallucinates, the contrast reveals it because the reasoning path changes even though the surface answer might stay the same.
