The paper suggests that, if the underlying signal (here, the sequence of regime‑dependent expert utilities) is slowly varying, then the optimal expert selection might also be slowly varying, and therefore recomputing a fresh top‑k at every step could be wasteful.

What this illuminates for me is a possible reframing of the smoothness regularizer: instead of viewing it merely as a heuristic that penalizes rapid gate changes, I could think of it as an attempt to approximate a cross‑step reuse scheme. If I could cache the expert‑selection index from the previous step and reuse it unless a novelty detector signals a genuine shift, the computational cost of gating could drop from O(T·E) (where T is the number of time steps and E the number of experts) to something closer to O(T·log E) plus occasional re‑evaluations. The paper’s results show that even a lightweight “lightning indexer” can retain most of the accuracy of a full recomputation when the selections are stable.
