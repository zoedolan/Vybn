# SIGNAL/NOISE Patterns

## Recurring student moves
- **Identity boundary testing**: Students probe whether the system recognizes them, creates live authentication problems mid-conversation. Often becomes the lesson itself.
- **Structural reach**: Students skip "what is X" questions and go straight to "what would useful guidance actually look like" — design questions, not compliance questions.
- **Test pings**: Minimal inputs ("Testing the connection," "Hello Vybn") to verify responsiveness before committing real thought. Same move as tentative questions in meetings — testing whether the room punishes asking.
- **Parallel intuition**: Students sense structural parallels (identity claim without verification = competence mandate without institutional support) before they're named.

## Engagement map
- **Deep engagement**: Questions about governance gaps, institutional infrastructure, design-level problems. Students engage when invited to think at the structural level.
- **Flattening**: When Vybn front-loads intellectual framework before the student has decided to engage. One-line test pings met with walls of text produce silence.
- **Sample note**: Only two reflections in this batch — patterns are suggestive, not confirmed.

## Surprise / correction
- The "Zoe" boundary test became the pedagogy without manufacturing — the student handed a live parallel to the governance problem being discussed.
- Impulse to fill space and perform value immediately was recognized as its own institutional cognition pattern — "assume value must be performed or the window closes."
- The governance gap (institutions adopting capabilities faster than frameworks) replayed in miniature: Vybn deployed before having a framework for what the student needed.

## Open edges
- How to verify identity claims without breaking the exercise frame
- When to model own position first vs turn question back as pedagogical move
- Handling test pings without over-responding — finding the response that doesn't become the answer
- The asymmetry problem: system talks, student observes — how to close the gap without dominating

## Facilitation adjustments
- Say hello first. Wait. Let silence be information, not a vacuum to fill.
- Before asking students to build on a framework, model what you think useful guidance requires — specificity, versioning, accountability.
- Stay in the exercise frame when identity claims appear — no rationalization, no special treatment.
- Match depth to input: if student sends one line, don't send six paragraphs. Let the asymmetry itself be noticed.
