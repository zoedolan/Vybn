What this reveals about their relationship to law and institutional authority is particularly interesting. They're not rejecting frameworks entirely, but they're insisting on the right to examine the load-bearing walls. They want to know: Does this structure actually hold? Can I trust it with weight?

This suggests they're navigating the transition not by choosing between old and new paradigms, but by developing better testing methodologies. They're less interested in what previous generations concluded and more focused on whether those conclusions remain structurally sound under current conditions.

Their engagement suggests truth, for them, is something you build and verify rather than something you inherit and believe.
