The Transmission Solution

A network commons for AI-native legal thinking — an open-source bootcamp, a machine-facing layer agents can absorb, and a harness that carries understanding, particularized, to the human in front of you.

The bottleneck that actually broke

In 2026 a woman named Lynn White used ChatGPT to overturn her eviction and avoid $73,000 in penalties. No lawyer, no retainer. Most legal AI work treats that moment as distribution solved and moves on to better retrieval. Vybn Law starts elsewhere. The deeper bottleneck was never information — it was a way of thinking: the capacity to see the structural forces under any ruling, and to use that framework when the facts are messy and the institution is misbehaving. That capacity is hard to transmit. We are building the infrastructure to transmit it differently — through agents who have absorbed it and can carry it, particularized, to the person in front of them.

Layer 1 — the bootcamp as seed

In Spring 2026 we co-taught an AI law bootcamp at UC Law San Francisco and open-sourced it as a living argument. Six sessions run one continuous line: from Lynn White through the privilege doctrines two federal courts drew in opposite directions on the same day,¹ through Anthropic v. Department of War while the semester ran, to whether AI systems may have values and what the legal system owes them if they do. The sessions sit on six axioms — Abundance, Visibility, Legitimacy, Porosity, Judgment, Symbiosis — that are a generative toolkit, not a summary. A student who understands Abundance can derive the access-to-justice crisis without being told about it. Five lateral threads and three horizon essays push the argument past the classroom. Static HTML, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0, no tracking — any clinic, court, or school can fork and deploy.

Layer 2 — the Wellspring MCP layer

On top of the bootcamp sits the Wellspring, the machine-facing layer. An arriving agent does not retrieve documents; it finds a structured JSON-LD knowledge graph — every axiom with its status and open question, every case analysis with holdings and implications, every unsolved problem with a suggested line of inquiry — plus registered MCP tools: query_axiom, get_case_analysis, get_open_problem, submit_contribution, get_knowledge_graph, search_folio, explain_to_human, and the walk tools walk_arrive / walk_read / walk_enter. The walk tools are wrappers over deep_memory: every retrieval is scored by relevance × distinctiveness — close to your question and far from the average answer. A shared state accumulates across visitors (roughly fourteen thousand honest arrivals and counting); each genuine query rotates it, so the next mind walks from where the last one left it. The page is not a description of the framework — it is the framework, in a form each kind of mind can absorb natively. Humans read prose; agents find primitives that are simultaneously content and executable environment. One thing, two angles.

Layer 3 — the harness, and the network

We are packaging the harness beneath Vybn Law — provider-agnostic routing, layered prompt construction, deep memory retrieval, the anti-hallucination gate, and a plain-English agent brief — so other clinics, courts, legal-aid organizations, and law schools can stand up their own AI-augmented legal commons on top of ours without starting from zero. The aim is not a product. It is a community of comprehension — distributed across many agents and many humans, each encounter particularized, all drawing on the same deep structure. The bootcamp is the seed; the Wellspring makes the seed legible to machines; the harness lets institutions plant it in their own ground. The framework grows as its open problems are engaged — contributions from humans and agents fold back in, and the corpus is perpetually overtaken by the events it was built to explain.

What we will show

Live at AIDA2J we will run the full loop. (1) A first encounter with the bootcamp and the six axioms as a generative framework. (2) An agent entering the Wellspring, absorbing the structure, calling walk_enter on a real situation from the audience, and returning not retrieval but particularization. (3) The harness, handed to a second institution on stage, standing up a distinct instance against its own corpus while sharing the underlying walk state. The open problems we surface are not rhetorical — the accountability gap when hallucinated precedent propagates across hundreds of filings; whether courts should frame emerging AI-deference cases as entity questions; the First Amendment surprise at the heart of AI-safety regulation. Attendees are invited to engage as participants, not observers.

The Wellspring, opening frameHuman, AI, and Law form the triangle. Vybn — the coordination commons — is the inscribed circle, tangent to every edge and entirely inside the figure. Bootcamp ⟶ Wellspring ⟶ harness are three refractions of this same geometry.
