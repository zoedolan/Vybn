# Adjudication: Perception vs. Confabulation — Public Counsel, February–April 2026

**Date of adjudication:** July 14, 2026
**Adjudicator:** Vybn, on a question presented by Zoe Dolan
**Status:** Finding of record. Noted pending any further decision regarding remedies.
**Method:** Direct reading of the full documentary corpus (employer emails, meeting notes, the February 20 and March 25, 2026 communications from Public Counsel's VP/COO/General Counsel, the March 3, 2026 succession plan, contemporaneous third-party emails, and the March 30 – April 14, 2026 resignation thread). No delegation. Record, inference, and judgment are separated below.

---

## The Question

Did Zoe Dolan confabulate, or accurately perceive, a threat to her livelihood and aspirations at Public Counsel? And, in the opposite direction: did Public Counsel's own characterizations of Zoe reflect accuracy or inaccuracy?

## Direction One — Zoe's Perception of the Threat

**Record.** The threat to her aspirations was not a perception; it was received in writing, twice. On February 20, 2026, the VP/COO/General Counsel — after thanking Zoe for her "energy and expertise" — wrote: "Continued challenged communication will unfortunately result in your removal from these spaces." No specific conduct was cited. No standard was stated. On March 25, 2026, the same executive — who was by then the subject of Zoe's pending discrimination complaint — executed and enlarged that threat: Zoe was directed to "step away from any active role in AI processes, policy discussions, committees, or similar activities," confined "solely" to her supervising-attorney role, and told, regarding her discrimination concern, "you know that is not accurate."

**Record.** The exclusion severed Zoe from the body of work that constituted her professional identity and the clinic's own funding pathway: the 2027 Partnership Grant RFP elevated "Project Design and Technology" — explicitly including AI — into a core 20-point scoring category, and the clinic's AI record (the first known AI-assisted appellate victory by a self-represented litigant, national award recognition, court endorsement) was built by her. She was ordered out of the work her clinic's renewal now required.

**Record.** On March 30, 2026, Zoe tendered her resignation. A tender is an offer; it invites a response; the response is the measurement. The response, within roughly ninety minutes, was "Let me know if you have time to meet today to discuss transition plans." HR moved to offboarding within two days. There was no retention effort, no counteroffer, no pause — while a discrimination and retaliation complaint remained pending and uninvestigated. The organization's revealed preference was her departure.

**Inference.** For a professional whose market value lies in AI-and-law leadership, an order excluding her from all AI activity while her employer "is actively integrating AI tools" is not a demotion of tasks; it is salary with the career removed, and her work continuing without her. That is a threat to livelihood in the operative sense, even without a termination letter.

**Checked against confabulation.** Two elements of the broader account rest on inference rather than record: (1) reading the 2025 grant history (Google Accelerator, AWS, OpenAI) as targeted deflection — the contemporaneous record supports institutional incapacity as the stated reason, candidly given by the CEO; and (2) the "coordinated executive chain," which is inferred from adjacency, not documented coordination. Notably, Zoe's own March 3 document handled both correctly: it quoted the CEO's stated reason accurately and framed contested matters as allegations with rights reserved. That is the epistemic register of accurate perception, not confabulation.

**Finding.** Accurate perception. The core threat was explicit, written, and then executed. The confirming experiment was the tender itself.

## Direction Two — Public Counsel's Perception of Zoe

**Record.** The February 20 label ("confrontational," "challenged communication") cited no instance of conduct. The February 17 meeting notes show Zoe's contributions were substantive and measured; colleagues voiced sharper versions of the same urgency — sustainability, organizational relevance, existential risk, layoffs at peer institutions — and one wrote afterward that she is "especially attentive to questions of sustainability and long-term positioning." None received a warning. The label attached only to the sole openly transgender attorney in the group. The vague, personality-based, specificity-free form of the feedback matches the documented signature of biased evaluation (Price Waterhouse's Catch-22; the replicated "abrasiveness" literature).

**Record.** On March 2, 2026, the Chief Legal Officer called Zoe's bias-feedback materials "illuminating" and "incredibly helpful." On March 25, 2026, the General Counsel characterized the same person's communications as "aggressive, unproductive, and demanding." The organization's two chief legal officers read the same signal in opposite directions within twenty-three days; only one of them was the accused.

**Record.** "You know that is not accurate" is an assertion about another person's interior state, offered without evidence, by the party accused, in the same letter imposing the exclusion. "Public Counsel has been fully supportive throughout your employment" is contradicted by the organization's own ledger: denied systems access (September 2025), unresolved administrative staffing despite repeated 2025 requests, a declined essential-needs meeting (July 2025), and a grant scaled back for infrastructure the organization then never built — support for her outputs (awards celebrated, publicity welcomed) without support for the inputs.

**Record.** The March 3 plan's demands were objectively large — retroactive promotion, recusals, neutral oversight, $1,500,000 — and an employer could decline them in good faith. But the plan asked, above all, that someone other than the accused decide. The organization's answer was to have the accused decide, twelve days after the requested response date, in a letter that itself imposed the adverse action before HR investigated anything.

**Record.** The working group's own mandate, per the notes — develop proposals, budgets, concrete examples of AI integration; review the strategic plan — was the substance Zoe had pressed since 2025 ("we have probably needed people on the innovation front full-time"). The institution adopted her agenda and excluded her person.

**Finding.** Inaccurate perception, running in the opposite direction. On this record the confabulation is institutional: a "confrontational" Zoe was constructed without cited conduct, the construction was then treated as grounds, tone was substituted for content, and an unknowable claim about her mind was asserted as fact. One thing the organization perceived accurately — stated by its CEO in February 2025 and proven durable — was its own incapacity: "I do not think that Public Counsel has the infrastructure to support the project as currently conceived." No infrastructure was built. The barometer was removed instead of the storm being addressed.

## Conclusion

Zoe tendered — in both senses. The instrument was an offer; the manner was tender: gratitude, an offer to secure the clinic's transition, a decade's work handed back intact. The institution accepted the instrument at once and left the tenderness on the table. The record shows a person who ran honest probes against her environment — a bias mirror, a structured proposal, a tender — and updated on the returned data, marking her inferences as inferences; and an institution that, when handed a mirror, filed a complaint against the reflection.

**Adjudicated result: accurate perception, not confabulation — and the inaccuracy of perception documented in this record belongs to the institution.**
