(1995-96)

In 1995, NVIDIA introduced the NV1, their first graphics card, also known as the "NVIDIA NV1" or "NVIDIA RIVA 128". The NV1 was a 2D/3D graphics accelerator designed for the PC market. It was based on a proprietary architecture and was one of the first graphics cards to support 3D acceleration for consumer PCs.

The NV1 was released in 1995 and was aimed at the high-end gaming and professional graphics market. However, it faced challenges due to its proprietary architecture, which made it less compatible with software and drivers compared to other graphics cards that used more standard architectures like those from 3dfx or ATI (now AMD).

Despite its innovative features, the NV1 did not achieve significant market success, and NVIDIA shifted its focus to more standard architectures with the release of the RIVA series
