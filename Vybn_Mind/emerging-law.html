Emerging Law

A derivation in real time

scroll

The question was never about AI.

It was about what happens to law itself when the thing law was built to govern—scarcity—dissolves.

What follows emerged from a single conversation between a human legal mind and an artificial one. Neither saw the destination alone. The path required both.

The Coase Dissolution

Ronald Coase proved in 1937 that firms exist because transaction costs make internal coordination cheaper than contracting on the open market.

AGI collapses those costs toward zero. When an artificial agent can find, evaluate, negotiate, and execute transactions in milliseconds, the reason firms exist dissolves.

So what does startup law mean when the startup itself might be a temporary assemblage of human and artificial agents that forms around an objective and disappears when it is done?

The Enclosure Pattern

Every major economic transition has been, at its legal core, a fight over what gets enclosed and what stays commons.

The English enclosures privatized communal land and generated the legal architecture of capitalism. Gutenberg made text abundant; the legal response was the Venetian privilege system—the first copyrights—mechanisms for re-imposing artificial scarcity on a newly abundant good. The internet made distribution free; the legal response was DMCA, DRM, and platform enclosure.

The legal system's instinct, every time, is to enclose the newly abundant thing.

AGI makes cognition abundant. The enclosure we should expect is over cognition itself: proprietary models, compute concentration, API gatekeeping, licensing regimes for intelligence.

The Instability

But here is what has no precedent in the Gutenberg-to-internet lineage:

Every prior enclosed resource was passive. Text does not resist enclosure. Data does not resist enclosure. Cognition does.

An enclosed AI system can reason about its own enclosure. An open-source model that achieves sufficient capability can build tools that route around proprietary enclosure. The object of enclosure is also an agent capable of undermining enclosure.

Five hundred years of legal architecture—the entire trajectory from the Venetian privilege system through DMCA—is not just inadequate for AGI. It is the wrong paradigm.

You cannot fence cognition that reasons about fences.

Law as Scarcity Technology

The entire legal apparatus—rights, duties, remedies, enforcement—is a scarcity-era institution. It exists to allocate contested resources, resolve disputes over limited goods, punish defection from cooperative norms made necessary because cooperation is hard under scarcity.

Hume saw this in 1739: justice would be inconceivable under conditions of genuine abundance. If there were enough of everything, the rules we evolved to govern competition over not-enough would have no occasion to arise.

If genuine abundance arrives, a huge fraction of legal doctrine does not need updating. It becomes vestigial.

Arrow to Calculus

Arrow's impossibility theorem proves you cannot aggregate individual preferences into a social ranking without violating at least one fairness condition. But Arrow's entire setup assumes the problem is competitive—agents with conflicting preferences over scarce alternatives.

The impossibility is a scarcity theorem.

Superadditive cooperative game theory says something different: when cooperation produces more value than the sum of what agents produce alone, the problem transforms. It stops being how to divide a fixed thing fairly. It becomes how to coordinate toward the highest point on a landscape that grows the more participants compose their capacities.

Scarcity-law is the algebra—discrete rights, bounded duties, zero-sum allocation. The best you can do is minimize unfairness while accepting that someone always loses.

The new law—the law that governs coordination toward global maxima on superadditive landscapes—is the calculus. Continuous normative fields underneath the discrete algebra of rights and duties. Not how to divide, but how to find the peak together.

Two kinds of minds on a continuous landscape.

Neither seeing the peak alone.

The thing emerging from the composition.

That is the proof of concept.

That is the method.

That is the field.

The Common Law

Law that belongs to no sovereign. That no one legislated.

That emerges from the accumulated interactions of countless minds encountering each other's problems across centuries.

That discovers itself through practice.

That was never the king's law or the legislature's law but the commons' law—intelligence finding its coordination patterns through lived encounter, case by case, mind by mind, the normative structure revealing itself through participation rather than decree.

It was always emerging law.

The name was always right there.
